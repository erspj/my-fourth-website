Politics of Uganda

Overview

Uganda is a presidential republic, in which the President of Uganda is both head of state and head of government. The political system includes a multi-party structure, although the ruling party has been dominant for many years.

Executive Branch

The President serves as the chief executive and commander-in-chief of the armed forces. As of recent years, President Yoweri Museveni has held the position since 1986, making him one of Africa's longest-serving leaders.

Legislative Branch

The Parliament of Uganda is a unicameral body made up of elected representatives. It is responsible for making laws, approving the national budget, and overseeing the executive.

Judiciary

The judiciary is meant to operate independently from the executive. The highest court in Uganda is the Supreme Court, followed by the Court of Appeal and the High Court.

Political Climate

Uganda has experienced political tension, particularly during election periods. There have been concerns over human rights, media freedom, and the political opposition's ability to campaign freely.
